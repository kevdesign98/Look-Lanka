High Alert

The "Closed" Temple Scam

"Sorry sir, today is a local holiday. This temple is closed until 4 PM."

The Trap

A friendly driver diverts you to a "free" festival which ends up being a high-pressure gem shop.

The Solution

Kindly say "I'll check the gate myself" and walk to the official entrance. Openings are 24/7 for tourists.

Very Common

Tuk-Tuk Price Inflation

"The meter is broken, but don't worry, 2000 LKR is the standard price."

The Trap

Drivers near tourist spots like Sigiriya or Mirissa charge 4× the local rate.

The Solution

Use the PickMe or Uber apps to check the fair price, even if you don't book through them.

High Alert

The Milk Powder Scam

"I don't need money, just buy some milk for my baby."

The Trap

You buy overpriced milk at a specific shop. The woman returns it and splits the cash with the owner.

The Solution

Offer to buy a banana or bread from a different stall. Usually, they will lose interest immediately.

👩 Women's Travel

Solo Female Travel Tips

The "Nice Guy" on the Train

Men might try to sit very close or initiate long personal conversations. It's perfectly fine to wear headphones (even without music) to signal you don't want to talk. On buses, try to sit next to other women.

Evening Commutes

Avoid walking alone on dark beaches or unlit streets after 9 PM. Use PickMe to book a Tuk-Tuk even for short distances — it's GPS-tracked and much safer than flagging one on the road.
