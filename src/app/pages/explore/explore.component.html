Kandy: Temple of the Tooth

"Where history meets spirituality in the misty hills."

Honest Tip

The evening ceremony (Theva) is loud and crowded. For a peaceful visit, go at 5:30 AM. Dress code is strict: shoulders and knees must be covered.

🍲 Eat like a local

Honest Flavours

Don't get tricked by "Tourist Menus". Here's what you must try and how much you should pay for it in an authentic restaurant.

Kottu Roti

600 - 900 LKR

The King of street food. Shredded roti bread, vegetables, eggs, and spices. If you hear a rhythmic metallic sound on the street, they are preparing it.

Must Try Spicy

Egg Hoppers

150 - 300 LKR

Thin bowl-shaped crêpes made with coconut milk and rice flour, with an egg in the center. Perfect for breakfast or a light dinner.

Cheap Eat Vegetarian

Rice & Curry

500 - 1200 LKR

The national meal. A mountain of rice accompanied by 5-6 different types of curry. In a real local "hotel," refills are often free.

Authentic Filling

Ceylon Tea

100 - 250 LKR

The most famous tea in the world. Enjoy it plain or as "Milk Tea" with condensed milk. Avoid tourist tea rooms: the best is served at roadside stalls.

🛡️ Travel Smart

The Anti-Scam Toolkit

Sri Lanka is a paradise, but like any gem, it has its little thieves. Here's how to avoid getting scammed and travel like a pro.

Tuk-Tuk Meters

In Colombo, always use **PickMe** or **Uber**. Outside the city, always ask "Meter?" before getting in. If they say it's broken, negotiate or find another driver.

Honest Price: 100-150 LKR / km

"It's Closed Today"

A classic scam in Kandy or Colombo. Someone will tell you the temple is closed for a holiday and offer to take you to a "secret" elephant festival. **It's false.**

Tip: Walk straight and check for yourself.

The Gem Factory

Your driver will "stop for gas" right in front of a jewelry store. They receive huge commissions for bringing you there. Only buy gems from certified shops.

Warning: "Incredible deals" do not exist.
